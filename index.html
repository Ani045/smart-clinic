Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for weight loss surgery?

+

Patients with a BMI over 37 or above 32 with obesity-related conditions like diabetes or hypertension are eligible. The surgery is considered for those who haven’t achieved significant weight loss through diet and exercise.

What are the different types of bariatric surgery?

+

Common procedures include Gastric Bypass, Sleeve Gastrectomy, and Mini Gastric Bypass. Each method has distinct advantages based on a patient's medical history and weight loss goals.

What are the long-term benefits of metabolic surgery?

+

Metabolic surgery not only aids in weight loss but also improves or resolves conditions like type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and sleep apnea. It significantly enhances overall quality of life.

What is robotic bariatric surgery?

+

Robotic bariatric surgery is a minimally invasive technique using robotic arms for precision. It ensures faster recovery, less pain, and minimal scarring for patients undergoing weight loss procedures.

How soon can I resume work after surgery?

+

Most patients can return to work within 2-3 weeks, depending on the type of surgery and individual recovery progress. Lighter activities can be resumed within a few days post-surgery.

How do I prepare for weight loss surgery?

+

Patients should follow a pre-surgery diet, avoid smoking, and undergo necessary medical evaluations. Mental preparedness and lifestyle changes are crucial for a successful surgery outcome.

What post-surgery diet should I follow?

+

A gradual transition from liquids to soft foods, then to a balanced diet, is recommended. High-protein, low-carb, and nutrient-rich meals help sustain weight loss and ensure long-term success.
